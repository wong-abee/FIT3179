Global Food Trends Over the Years

Embark on a visual exploration of global food trends, where data paints a vivid picture of culinary shifts and economic realities. This visualization offers insights into how global prices have fluctuated over the years and it's a journey through the intersection of affordability, nutrition, and the evolving palate of our planet, revealing the dynamic forces shaping our food choices and their impact on our world.

Price Trends

In 2021, the global daily cost of a healthy diet, measured in international dollars, highlighted a notable revelation. Australia emerged as one of the countries with lower costs, in stark contrast to the global average of approximately $3-4. This disparity underscores significant variations in food affordability and nutrition worldwide.

Global commodity prices, spanning 1950 to 2020, reveal a dynamic economic landscape. Notably, beef and lamb emerge as the most expensive commodities, with prices measured relative to real prices in 1900 (set at 100).

Affordability Trends

This visualisation represents food expenditure per person from 2017-2021, focusing solely on food bought for home consumption. It excludes out-of-home purchases, as well as expenses on alcohol and tobacco. All values are presented in US dollars and are not adjusted for inflation or variations in the cost of living across countries.

Affording a healthy diet

India records the highest percentage of its population among the countries shown who are unable to afford a healthy diet, in stark contrast to Switzerland, where the figure is 0%. Over the years from 2017 to 2021, these percentages exhibit minimal fluctuations, highlighting persistent disparities in food affordability worldwide.
